Introduction?????????

To oscillate back and forth between being fueled with inspiration and coming up with new ideas is my creative dream! The creative cycle is the cycle of taking in fun ideas and churning out new fun stuff.

I like to think that in order for something meaningful to come out of the creative machine, something meaningful has first to go into the the creative machine. To keep the flow of creativity going, I have to keep feeding myself with new and exciting things.

I like to imagine that my brain is a very large billiards table with millions of billards balls. In order for interesting things to happen, there needs to be kinetic energy put into the system. That kinetic fuel comes in the form of meaningful and comprehensible input. When this happens, I get inspired.

If I consume something that isn’t meaningful or comprehensible, it goes in one ear, passes between all the billards balls, hardly rattling a thing, and goes right out the other ear. In order for an exchange of kinetic energy from the outside world to the billiard balls in my brain to occur, it has to be something that I intrinsically and immediately find value and excitment in! I have to allow myself to be having as much fun as possible.

There are two main topics to discuss here: the learn drive and the processes involved in the creative cycle.

The creative cycle

I like to think of the creative cycle as a 2x2 matrix consisting of (analytic, creative) × (input, output)! Namely, there is an analytic and creative aspect to getting creative fuel into your brain, and there is a creative and analytic aspect to getting new creative ideas back out of your brain.

The learn drive is like a naturally occurring spaced-repetition algorithm implemented by the brain itself! The brain knows when its time to revisit some topic, because it leaves you itching to get back to it! And the brain knows when its time to give it a rest, because you start to get bored! Learn drive is tightly coupled with focus and distraction and with sprints.

Learning, consumption, and creativity

There is a very tight connection between explicitly learning new things, consuming things, and creativity.

When you consume something, if it’s meaningful that means you are actively finding ways to relate it to your world of things you care about. In other words you are actively embedding the new content into your neural web of knowledge and experiences: you are learning! In this learning you are actively increasing your breadth and/or depth of appreciation by forming new ideas on the outskirts of your knowledge, which paves the way for even more learning. And as this happens, you are simultaneously fueling the cogs of creativity! Your creative voices kick into action and you naturally have things to say about what you are enjoying! You might think you are able to enjoy something without being creative about it. But the way I see it, if you like it, you already have something to say about it! What do you like about it? That is an artifact of creativity. You and you alone were able to find value in what you experienced, and that takes creativity! That requires that you were able to fit it into your network of goodness, and you did it. When in doubt, just start talking about what you like about it, and as you keep talking abut it, you’ll find more and more things to say about it. And this doesn’t have to be in the form of words, either! It can be in the form of imagery or sounds!

Here’s an experiment I love: next time you hear a new melody that captivates you, after the music is over, take a moment of silence, and let the melody play back in your memory. Don’t worry about remembering it accurately, just remember what you can, not for the sake of remembering it per se, but for the sake of relishing in the echos of the joy that you just felt. And if you start to imagine a melody that you know is different from the one you heard, let yourself! This is creativity! You are already beginning to generalize what you found valuable about what you just heard to something new and just as valuable!

Learning and creativity are natural consequeces of consuming meanigful things. Explicit learning can be said as well to be about rigorously consuming new and meaingful things such that you absorb it inside and out. But notice that you can’t do this without creativity. Learning is inherently a creative endeavor.

So there you have it, learning, consumption, and creativity are all facets of the same overall process!

Scheduling

How do you know when to consume and when to create? The learn drive tells you! When you are drooling with ideas and overwhelmed with things to say, it’s clearly time to create! And when you are eager for a particular experience and can’t wait, it’s time to consume! Sometimes though it’s not so clear, and you’re in more of a neutral state. In times like this, maybe that’s fine. Maybe times like this you can spend doing marathons instead.

Here’s a rule of thumb:

  • When you have something to say, say it!

    Most notibly, even if you don’t have time to say it full, at least jot it down for later!

  • When you have something to see, see it!
  • When you aren’t sure what to do, just enjoy the process of the marathon.

There’s another thing you can do when you just aren’t leaning either way. If you aren’t feeling particularly creative, but you want to feel creative, then you can start the creative engines! And how do you do that? By feeding yourself creative fuel! Alternatively, you can look inside yourself and stimulate all of the exciting ideas dorment within you without external stimulus via image streaming.

Creative input

I like to break the processes of getting creative fuel into my brain into an analytic part and a creative part: the (likely subconscious) parsing of the information (figuring out what it actually is) and the evaluation and assimilation of it in the context of everything I care about.

Analytic parsing

There is no such thing as “pure information”. Information is inseparable from its encoding! There is always some arrangement of symbols in addition to the meaning found in it.

In this context, I like to think of a spectrum of encodings that ranges from external, when the information is outside your brain, to internal, where the information is inside your brain. Information encoding is continuously being transformed from one end to the other as it passes between our brains and the outside world.

On the extreme external end of the spectrum we have the physical encodings which are suitable for our senses to receive. For example, an image is encoded in the physical world as a pattern of light that can be meaningfully received by our eyes, and natural language is encoded as sound pressure waves that can be meaningfully received by our ears.

Our senses then take convert these physical world encodings into the very beginnings of neural encodings. And the information continues to be filtered up through a series of neural transformations from our lower level sensory nervous system to our cognition.

This series of transformations from the low level physical encoding to the high level cognitive encoding is what constitutes the analytic parsing process.

When I say that its important for the kinetic fuel to be comprehensible, this is what I mean. If it’s not comprehensible, then the analytic parsing stage fails! The brain simply isn’t able to convert the information from low level signals into meaningful higher level signals that we can think about!

A matrix of processes

Notice that there are two processes related to input and two processes related to output. And notice that there are two creative processes and two analytic processes! Each of the four processes can be put into a 2x2 matrix!

The pillars of the creative cycle

I sort my projects into these categories, as distinguished in the primary role the play in the creative cycle:

Consumption (Input)
Pieces of media I want to consume or experiences I want to have!
Learning (Assimilation)
Knowledge to acquire and skills to develop!
Creativity (Output)
Objects of external self-realization!

Of course things don’t fall perfectly into these categories! In fact, all projects involve some degree of all of these things. For example, to consume something meaningful is inevetibly to learn something and to fuel the cogs of creativity, and to realize your creative ideas with real words and other crafts is to solidify your own understanding.
