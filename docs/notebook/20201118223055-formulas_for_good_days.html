Formulas for Good Days

How to have a good day!

The idea of sprints here is super connected with learn/creative drive.

This formula inspires my daily structure.

Why do I like having a formula for this? Because if it works, and if I stick to it, I can minimize dissatisfaction with the day, and maximize my satisfaction with the day!

My original thought a while ago of a formula for a good day was this:

  • finish something
  • work a little on something longterm
  • start something new and unique in some way

I think this was hitting on some good ideas, but I think it’s a little foggy.

So here’s my new formula (to be done in order!):

  1. Focus intensely on one predetermined project: (the sprint)
  2. Gently nurture a few predetermined long term goals: (the marathon)
  3. Relax and tend to anything I feel like: (the leisure time)

I like thinking about this in terms of the sprint, the marathon, and the leisure time!

The reasonings behind the new formula are this:

  • Step one, the sprint, satisfies the feeling of urgency, of needing to get things done, it allows me to put a lot of energy to make great strides quickly towards something that I should be able to finish in a relatively short period of time
  • Step two, the marathon, satisfies the need of non-neglegence towards the many things I want to do. I can always feel like I’m progressing in many things at once, and I am!
  • Step three, the leisure time, satisfies the spontaneous urges to indulge in random projects. It’s a way of relaxation because I’m allowing myself to do whatever is most comfy at that time, and it’s a really good way to take a break from the things I do everyday.
  • As for the ordering, I want to allocate fresh morning energy towards the more heavily focused endeavors, therefore the things that require more energy and focus come at the start of the day. The sprint requires most energy and focus, so it comes first. The marathon requires less energy than the sprint, but more energy than the leisure time, because it’s still intentionally directed at predecided collection of things. And the leisure time comes naturally at the end of the day!
  • Interleaving multiple things throughout the day is always mutually benificial for the progress in all of the things because I dont risk burning out on any particular thing. And by spreading my attention over a longer period of time I aid the nurturing of long term memories.

With this formula I’ll append my basic daily agenda with slots for the sprint, the marathon, and the leisure time.

The new formula is similar to the old formula and captures its basic ideas, but does so in a more refined and specific way:

  • The old idea to “finish something” is in the spirit of the sprint: it’s completion oriented! The difference is that in the new formula, there’s not an expectation of finishing it specifically that day, just over a relatively short span of days.
  • The old idea of “working a little on something longterm” is in the spirit of the marathon: it’s process oriented!
  • The old idea of “starting something new and unique in some way” is sort of in the spirit of the leisure time in the sense that I usually start something new because of a spontaneous urge to do so!
