Stress Management

I deal with a lot of environmental stress!!!!!!!! I don’t have my own room, there’s no space to be alone in the house, there’s only one bathroom which everybody is fighting over all the time, everyone is constantly yelling and slamming doors because they are hard of hearing and don’t know better and I have to constantly guard my things so people don’t try and fiddle with them and they end up broken or missing or my clothes or dishes being washed with disgusting detergents that make me gag. And the dogs!!!!!! The barking is insidious!!!!! It’s the most annoying bark I’ve ever heard in my life too. And it’s at the most inoppoortune times in the middle of the night.

So! How to deal with this?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?

I think I’ve realized that making a gentle attempt to meditate the bubbles of anticipatory anxiety that follows from all of this seems to really make a difference! It’s hard to deal with because sometimes I get so angry at everything because I don’t have control over the things that I need control over! But I think consciously realizing that just letting the thoughts come and go can help diffuse that anger and anxiety prevents the excessive bubbling of stress and frustration that makes me throw my phone in the dirt and feel like my head’s going to explode.

Another thing that helps is finding ways to feel like I have control by focusing on the control I have over the future. I know I won’t be in this situation forever, and I know that what I do now will have a big impact down the line, so I can focus on doing things now that I’ll benefit from later.

There are also things that I can actually control right now to a reasonable degree, like the food I eat! I’m very happy with the progression of my diet this year. I’ve gone from eating whatever has been available (junk food and unhealthy everything) to eating more or less only the things I feel comfortable eating

Staying highly engaged with things I’m interested in helps a lot too. I often have a tendency to feel down in a rut when I have trouble sleeping well, but I have to remember all the times when I’ve stayed up way later than I should several days in a row due to being overwhelmingly obsessed with some project. Clearly I wasn’t sleeping well then, and yet I was having a good time. Not to say I should try to dismiss sleep like that, but that even if I do have sleep troubles, that’s not enough to stop me from doing the things I want to do.

I also feel good about the things I need in order to eventually take better control over my sleeping. I need a night guard and I need a more peaceful environment. If I have those things I feel confident I’ll be able to reasonably take back control of my sleeping.

It’s also nice to stop and relax and enjoy little things sometimes. I like tea lately. And walking is honestly so good XD It’s like pacing but better! I can just get lost in my thoughts and talk to myself for an hour walking in big circles around the yard dispelling a lot of energy. It’s comfy in the way zoning out to a warm shower is comfy.

I want to try and really emphasise step three of Formulas for Good Days, making time towards the end of the day to relax and tend to anything I want to at all. I feel really good about the idea of just crawling comfy in bed after doing a lot of satisfying work and just writing like this or watching category theory videos or something lol
