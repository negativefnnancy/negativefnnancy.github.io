Adapting to mental status

When I say “mental status” I mean the spectrum from when I am feeling really good and stimulated with lots of hyper energy to when I am feeling really bleh and low energy and just not up to doing much but staying in bed.

I find that in order to reliably follow a routine, my mental status has to be just right. If I am too high, I am far too invested in and distracted by this really cool thing that’s got me super excited to pay attention to a complicated routine which sucks away all that time I could be spending diong the really cool thing. And if I’m too low, I just don’t have the energy to bother.

At a first attempt, I plan only for my “ideal” mental status, when I am in the middle, which is when I’m stable and best able to follow a routine. But this leads to frustration when inevitable fluctations occur! My second and better attempt, is to adapt my routine to my mental status. This way, I can make the most out of my day no matter how it is that I’m feeling that day.

So I have multiple routines depending on my mental status. Currently I have a standard routine and a low routine. But first, I wantto talk about how similar being high and being low actually is!

The symmetry of highs and lows: for every focus there is a distraction

I notice that when I’m off-center, regardless of which direction it is in, I have difficulties in maintaining a stable routine.

I find this pattern similar to the idea that focus and distraction are two sides of the same coin: to say you are focused on something is just to say you are distracted from something else! Or to frame it another way: when you are distracted from what you suppose you ought to be doing, you are focused on what you suppose you ought not be doing! In other words, focus and distract are essentially the same thing! The differece is that focused has a positive connotation while distracted has negative one.

Similarly, highs and lows are like times of focus and times of distraction. When I am high, it’s easy for me to pour buckets of energy into something I love, while simultaneously making it extremely difficult for me to do anything else, including other things I suppose I ought to be doing. When I am low, it’s extremely difficult for me to do the the things I suppose I ought to be doing, but it becomes really easy for me to dwell and lament on real concerns that I often feel out of control of.

Notice the similarity! In both cases, it’s extremely difficult for me to do the things I suppose I ought to be doing, namely, follow a steady routine. And in both cases my attention is consumed by something else which is keeping me from doing those other things. In the case of being high, it’s easy to use the word “focused”, because it’s easy to see it as a good and productive use of my attention. And in the case of being low, it’s easy to use the word “distracted”, because it’s easy to see it as a bad and draining use of my attention.

But I think that bad can be made good! It just takes some perspective: When I am low, I tend to gravitate towards specific characteristically low dwellings, such as staying in bed, comfort food, addressing my most basic of problems, or just getting a break from what I’ve been doing so much lately. But I think that at the heart of it, these are all essentially good things to address! The problems arise when they lead to unhealthy behaviours such as not getting enough exercise, eating junk food, becoming overrun with stress about things out of my control, and just being unproductive for too long.

The issue then becomes keeping these things under control so that they don’t veer into the bad category. Hence the low routine (below)! A routine that is adapted to pleasing my low brain without allowing it to derail too much.

My routines

So here are descriptions of (and links to) my current routines.

Standard routine

My original high and low routines were adapted to be minimal so that I could still aim to take care of myself in basic ways (such as nutrition and sleep) while still not asking too much of myself. I saved the bulk of routine work for my stable routine.

Now I realized that I actually like the minimalism of my high routine and so have merged my high and middle routines into a flexible standard routine. The way this routine adapts for the range between high and middle is with sprints.

Sprints are my goals that manifest from the energy of a high mental status. When I get super duper excited about something and just want to put gallons of energy into it, I make it a sprint. I keep it a sprint until it’s either completed or I get bored (learn drive)!

Everything else remains a marathon. Something that I can more relaxingly engage in the process of doing a little bit everyday. I’ll allow myself to do as much marathon work a day as I feel like, as long as I do a little, to leave space for any sprints I may have. And if I have no sprints, I’ll spend all the time on marathons!. I’ll use pomodoros to help when I’m less inclined to get into the marathons!

Low routine

Sometimes I just don’t feel like doing much, and even keeping up with my standard routine, even though it has been made more minimal than the original, is just too much work. So I base this routine on appeasing myself without going over board and finding ways to still remain satisfyingly productive even in a state like this. I strip away aspects of my standard routine that I just don’t feel like dealing with and emphasise comforts and leisure time to give me a boost.

I find that the comfort of staying in bed on my actually helps me to remain productive in a mind-wandering sort of way. I usually end up relaxing and doing things that are entirely off schedule but still valuable to me.

I do restrict my desire for comfort food though. Food has immense impact on the way I feel and so I must stick to my foodsies plan! But I can certainly eat as much as I want!

Spending a lot of time socializing and catching up with friends is also a nice productive and refreshing way to spend this time.
