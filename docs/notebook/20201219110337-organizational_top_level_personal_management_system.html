Attributes of activities

I want to think about different kinds of activities:

input/output
things you take in vs things you put out. what u take in might be consuming media, or reading, and is part of learning too. what u put out is what u say or create or synthesise urself and realize outside of urself
sprint/marathon
these relate to scheduling, allocating time and mental energy. sprints are high intensity bursts of energy poured into an activity with minimal interruptions, compact and over a relatively short period of a few days or a week or two or so. a marathon is much less intensity, has more breaks between sessions, and can be indefinitely long term
goals/indefinite procesess
some actcivities are meant to be completed, others are meant to be continued on forever. goals would be like to complete a project, indefinite processes would be like to practice a skill and always be improving

Creative cycle

What’s the creative cycle? The creative cycle is the cycle of consuming something, getting inspired by it, having something to say, and then saying it! So it’s essentially the entire pipeline of mental functioning. Stuff goes in, and stuff comes out!

I want to apply the concept to all kinds of media. A simple example would be , say, i read something, get inspired, and write about my thoughts on it. But more generally, any experience or groups of experience can serve for any other kind of creative output.

Most generally, “creative output” refers to the manifestation of any kind of synthesis of ideas and experiences triggered by other ideas and experiences.

Spaced repetition generalization

So spaced repetition is about spacing out in increasing intervals when you review some material. The idea is to review right before you would otherwise forget it, in order to strengthen the long term consolidation of the material!

Implicit in this idea is that the spacing occurs over time. I want to generalize the concept to intervals over knowledge space as well as time!

I want to generalize a hypothesis for why spaced repetition works: My conjecture is that spacing aids memory consoldation by deepening the embedding in the network of knowledge: i.e. more associations. The reason time intervals work then, is because the active network regions in your brain vary over time, and by engaging at different times you are creating wider varieties of associations. “Neurons that fire together wire together.”

The idea then is that not only does engaging with the material across different time intervals correlate with increased efficiency in consolodation, but also engaging with the material across many different mental states, background states, and perspectives does as well! So for example, you would study the material initially, then you would study the material in a different location (especially a location you have never been to before and is unlike anything you have ever seen), then you would study the material after having a highly novel and exciting conversation, then you would study the material after reading about something completely seemingly irrelevant. You would study when you are mad, you would study when you are calm, you would study when you are in love. You would study it after playing a new video game. Etc etc etc!
