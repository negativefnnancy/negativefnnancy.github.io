Layer 1: activities and goals

These are the basic concepts that everything else builds on!

Activity

An activity is anything that can be clocked in and out of so that I can keep track of how much time and when I spend on it.

Goal

A goal is a concrete objective that has either been accomplished or not yet accomplished.

Layer 2: tasks, indefinite processes, and milestones

These are entities that are particular combinations of activities and goals!

Task

A task is simultaneously an activity and a goal!

Tasks may be recurring meaning that they may be specified once but yield multiple instances.

A task may recur at regular intervals of time, for example, daily or weekly, or it my recur in response to certain events, for example, to lock the door when you leave the house.

Indefinite process

An indefinite process (or just process for short) is an activity that is not a task, i.e. it is not a goal and can’t be accomplished.

Milestone

A milestone is a goal that is not a task, i.e., it is not an activity and can’t be clocked in or out of. Instead, it is simply based around a condition!

Project

A project is a collection of milestones, processes, and tasks.

A consumption project is a project to consume some media, e.g. a book, a show, some music, a game, etc.. It’s primarily characterized by tasks that clock the time spend consuming and mark the completion of consumption. I want to find a way to formally articulate “active consumption,” where, in conjuction with all input, is some kind of output as well…. so like I might want to watch a show and then write a review, or something!!!

A learning project is a project to develop a skill or obtain some knowledge. It’s primarily characterized by tasks to consume learning materials, activities to log practice/training/contemplation, and milestones indicating learning goals.

A creative project is a project to produce some tangible output. It’s primarily characterized by tasks that clock the time spent working towards realizing the desired product and possibly activities to clock the time spent revising beyond initial goals.

Layer 4: sprints, marathons, spaced repetition, the creative cycle, and portfolios

And this builds on the layer 3 concepts!

Sprint

A sprint is a type of schedule that involves allocating large blocks of time in the day towards a single activity possibly for many consecutive days in a row. This is a type of schedule that is useful for effectively allocating energy that arises from big mental stimulation.

Marathon

A marathon is a type of schedule that involves allocating smaller bocks of time consistently and recurringly over a longer period of a time. This is more suited for maintaining a steady flow of activity and forming habits.

Spaced repetition

Spaced repetition is a type of schedule built around incremental intervalic recursion. I want to extend the idea to learn-drive saturation induced time dilation and “spatial” intervals. I want to apply it not only to discrete memory recall, but also dynamic skill practice and motivational/learn-drive/aappreciation modeling.

The creative cycle

The creative cycle is a cycle of an input activity followed by an output activity. For example, I may repeatedly listen to music and then compose. The idea is that by consuming first you gain inspiration for what you create afterwards.

Portfolio

A portfolio is a collection of projects that I choose to showcase! It’s probably mostly gonna be creative projects. The goal here is to have a set of shiny artifacts of self realization that I can water and tend to and make the best I can make them!

Layer 5: agendas

Agenda

An agenda is a collection of tasks that recur according to a schedule. For example, I might have a few daily agendas to organize tasks for me to do every day that follow a daily schedule, and maybe a weekly agenda of things to do each week that follows a weekly schedule. I may also have agendas for marathons and agendas for sprints.
