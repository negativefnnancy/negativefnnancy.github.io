Notes on “learning how to learn” course on coursera

Chunking
the process of bringing scattered ideas and mini-chunks together to form a new chunk which can be referred to with a single slot of working memory
Einstellung
when you are blinded to a new way of thinking by an old, comfortable and familiar way of thinking
Interleaving
when you mix up different areas of study within a single study session, which allows you to build creativity and independent thinking, this is the “top down” aspect of learning, where chunking is the “bottom up” aspect
Recall
actively recalling the material on your own is the most effective way to solidify the new material in your mind as compared with highlighting and concept mapping
Overlearning
when you spend excessive amount of time on a single area of the subject in a single study session; it has its uses, for example, it helps to develop subconscious automation (which may be useful for beating nervousness), but you quickly get diminishing returns towards the goal of solidifying the material in long term memory, for which its much more effective to repeat the material over a long period of time
